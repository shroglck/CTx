Abstract

Recent works have shown that current state of the art models are not able to generalize to occlusion in image domain. In this work we show the lack of generalization of current CNN and transformer models to the task of video action recognition and propose two benchmark datasets. We call them UCF-101-O and Kinetics-400-O. To solve, inspired by the success of compositional models , as well as Transformers to partial occlusion in image domain, we propose a transformer based Compositional network, which we call CTx-Net.We conduct various experiments on UCF 101 , first 101 classes of Kinetics 400 , the proposed UCF 101-O and Kinetics 400 O models to show the robustness of this model to partial occlusion.Our proposed model manages to outperform the base models by a significant margin.We also study the comparison of the performance of our model to models which have been trained using strong data augmentation.We also conduct in depth experiments to understand how properties of the occluder - motion , size or type affects the performance of our model as well as current models
